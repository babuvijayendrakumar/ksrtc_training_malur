SAFETY REFERENCE

RTO Traffic Signs & Signals

Standardised road signals defined by the Indian Road Congress (IRC) to ensure safe traffic flow and avoid collisions.

Traffic Light Signals

RED SIGNAL

Stop completely. You must stop before the stop line or zebra crossing. Never cross or jump a red signal.

AMBER / YELLOW

Clear intersection. Stop if you can do so safely. If you have already crossed the line, proceed with caution.

GREEN SIGNAL

Go ahead. Proceed straight or turn left/right if the lane is clear. Remain vigilant for pedestrian traffic.

SAFETY DIRECTIVE

Basic Driving Rules & Guidelines

Standard guidelines taught at KSRTC Central Training Institute to promote disciplined and defensive driving practices.

1. Lane Discipline & Indication

Always maintain your vehicle in the designated lane. Change lanes only after checking side-mirrors, indicating your intention via turn signals at least 30 meters in advance, and ensuring it is safe to proceed.

2. Right of Way Protocols

Yield to emergency vehicles (ambulances, fire engines) immediately. At roundabouts, yield to vehicles approaching from your right. Always yield to pedestrians at zebra crossings.

3. Overtaking Regulations

Overtake only from the right side of the vehicle ahead. Never attempt to overtake on blind curves, narrow bridges, junctions, or near school crossings where visibility is restricted.

4. Speed Management

Observe speed signs strictly. Reduce speed on wet roads, during heavy downpours, foggy conditions, and near school zones. Defensive driving mandates adjusting speed according to traffic flow.

5. Safe Following Distance

Always maintain a safe stopping distance behind the vehicle ahead. Implement the 2-second rule in normal weather, and double it to 4 seconds on wet roads or at night.

6. Zero Tolerance Violations

Driving under the influence of alcohol, using mobile phones while driving (even hands-free), and jumping traffic lights are heavy offenses carrying severe fines and license suspension.

CTI Driving Safety Pledge

"Seatbelts in cars and ISI helmets on two-wheelers are mandatory. Protect your life and respect fellow commuters. Safety is not a choice, it is a duty."
